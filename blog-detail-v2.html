How to Choose Pendant Lights for Restaurants: A Wholesale Buyer's Guide

Home  /  Blog  /  Pendant Lights for Restaurants
28 June 2026Buying Guides 6 min read

A dining pendant does two jobs at once: it lights the plate, and it sets the mood of the entire room. Get either wrong and the space feels flat — too bright and clinical, or too dim to read a menu. This guide covers the four factors that decide whether a pendant works in a real restaurant, and how to specify each one when ordering from a factory.

1. Ceiling Height & Drop Length

The bottom of a dining pendant should sit 75–90cm above the table surface. Lower, and tall guests block sightlines; higher, and the pool of light spreads too wide, killing intimacy. Always order pendants with field-adjustable cables — ceiling heights vary across a dining floor, and your installer will thank you.

  • 2.6–2.8m ceilings: compact pendants, Ø18–36cm, single drop
  • 3m+ ceilings: statement rings or cascade clusters fill the vertical space
  • Booths & banquettes: wall-mounted reading lights beat pendants here

2. Color Temperature (CCT)

2700K is the restaurant standard — warm enough to flatter food and faces, close to candlelight. 3000K works for brighter, casual concepts. Anything above 3500K reads as fast-food. For venues that shift from lunch service to evening ambience, specify dim-to-warm LED modules that slide from 3000K down to 2200K as they dim.

"Guests never notice good lighting — they just notice the food looks wonderful and everyone at the table looks relaxed."

3. Glare Control

Exposed diodes are the most common failure in cheap fixtures. Look for deep-recessed light sources, opal diffusers, or indirect designs where LEDs bounce off an inner reflector. At the spec-sheet level, ask the factory for a UGR value or simply request a real photometric test photo taken from seated eye height.

4. Serviceability & Compliance

Restaurants run their lighting 12+ hours a day. Specify replaceable drivers (not potted-in), and confirm CE/RoHS documentation before shipment — customs will ask, and so will your client's insurer. A factory that tests 100% of fixtures before packing will save you far more than the price difference of the cheapest quote.

Spec Checklist for Your Next Order

  • Drop height 75–90cm above table, adjustable cable
  • CCT 2700–3000K, CRI≥90, dim-to-warm optional
  • Recessed or diffused source — no visible diodes seated
  • Replaceable driver · CE & RoHS docs · 100% pre-shipment test
